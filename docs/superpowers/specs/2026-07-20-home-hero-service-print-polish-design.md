# C&C Home Hero and Service Chapter Polish

## Goal

Give the Home opening the restrained photographic polish of the approved Leica reference while preserving the existing factual copy, routes, service data, and interactions.

## Hero

- Retain the existing full-bleed `detail-process` photograph and responsive source.
- Remove the heavy page-wide dark scrim. Use the photograph at near-natural brightness.
- Crop the image deliberately so the vehicle remains the visual subject and the naturally dark lower-left area supports readable text.
- Reduce the Home headline from poster scale to a controlled campaign scale.
- Keep the headline, description, primary `/contact` quote action, and secondary `/portfolio` link exactly as written.
- Use wider negative space and precise lower-left placement instead of decorative overlays, gradients, cards, badges, or invented proof.
- Keep the hero image-led on mobile without forcing actions below an unnecessarily tall viewport.

## Service Chapter

- Change the full `service-board-section` to the existing warm-white `--print` surface used by the customer review.
- Use `--print-text` for headings and primary content, restrained warm-gray rules for structure, and red only for prices, focus, and active state.
- Keep the current four-service desktop selector and single-panel mobile accordion behavior.
- Keep all service names, descriptions, inclusions, options, and prices unchanged.
- Keep service photography in full color. Do not place the service stage inside a rounded card or dark container.
- Preserve the existing contact link after the selector, restyled for the print surface.

## Transition and Rhythm

- Let the bright service chapter meet the photographic hero with a clean hard edge, creating a deliberate material change.
- Avoid a transitional gradient, floating overlap, or decorative divider.
- The service chapter remains visually distinct from the later review through its interactive index, photography, and denser information rhythm, even though both use the same warm-white base.

## Responsive and Accessibility Requirements

- Preserve semantic headings, tab/tabpanel behavior, accordion state, keyboard navigation, visible focus, and all link destinations.
- Verify 390, 768, 1024, and 1440-pixel widths for readability, crop quality, header clearance, and horizontal overflow.
- Maintain one rendered service branch and one active service image at each responsive mode.
- Re-run focused Home/service tests, the full test suite, production build, browser interaction checks, and Lighthouse accessibility checks.

## Scope

This revision is limited to the Home hero and the immediately following service chapter. Portfolio, Contact, the review chapter, business facts, and approved wording remain unchanged.
